Dog

Follow this quick seven-step tutorial to learn how to draw a dog!

First, find a reference photo online of a dog. I found this one from Wikipedia!

Study your reference image until you have a good understanding of the animal you are about to draw.

Image by JOLLY-DOGS-JACK-RUSSELL, CC BY-SA 3.0, via Wikimedia Commons

Now that you have studied your image, begin drawing the head of your dog.

TIP: If you are having trouble starting, draw a circle as a base, then draw your ears and head shape. You can erase the circle after you are finished!

Once you are happy with your head shape, draw the body of the dog.

TIP: Try to keep your lines organic to increase the realism of your art!

Now that you have drawn the head and body, it's time to draw the front and back legs! Always check on your reference image if you need help.

Your dog's body is finished! Now, draw its face: include eyes, a nose, and a mouth. If you want to add detail, feel free to draw whiskers or fur marks.

Did you know? A dog's nose print, much like a human fingerprint, is one-of-a-kind! Find more fun dog facts from American Kennel Club here.

Great, you have finished the outline of your dog! Now, feel free to add any color or personality to your drawing to make it uniquely yours.

Did you know? There are more than 75 million pet dogs in the United States alone. Find more fun dog facts from American Kennel Club here.

You did it! Now you have a great piece of art that you can share with your friends and family. Feel free to return to steps in this tutorial, or go to another tutorial.
