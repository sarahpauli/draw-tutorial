Betta Fish

Follow this quick six-step tutorial to learn how to draw a Betta Fish!

First, find a reference photo online of a Betta Fish. I found this one from Wikipedia!

Study your reference image until you have a good understanding of the animal you are about to draw.

Image by Daniella Vereeken, CC BY 2.0, via Wikimedia Commons

Now that you have studied your image, begin drawing the body of your fish.

TIP: Your fish's body should look like an almond! Instead of drawing a perfect shape, try to add some lumps and bumps so that your drawing is organic and realistic.

On the back half of your fish's body, draw its tail. Follow an organic shape for the back fin, then draw lines spaced throughout the shape to show texture.

Bettas have dramatic, long tails, so take your time on this part. Try to follow the shape in the example - large, flowing, and wavy!

Now that you have the main shape of the Betta ready to go, add some details! Draw a gill, smaller fin, and eyeball... don't be afraid to give your fish some personality! Feel free to add anything else that will make your drawing feel unique.

Did you know? A Betta's stomach is as large as its eye! Find more fun Betta facts from bettafish.org here.

Now that you have an outline for your fish, color it in however you like! Feel free to add more details, like scales.

Did you know? A Betta's bright colors are not natural - purples, reds, blues, and pinks are all results of selective breeding. Find more fun Betta facts from bettafish.org here.

You did it! Now you have a great piece of art that you can share with your friends and family. Feel free to return to steps in this tutorial, or go to another tutorial.
